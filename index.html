Lessons from My Losers

My biggest investment mistakes and the valuable lessons learned. Failure is the best teacher in the markets.

ZOOM (ZM)
March 2020 - Dec 2021
-65%
Bought heavily during the pandemic thinking remote work was permanent. Held too long as the stock peaked at $550+ and rode it all the way down. Got caught up in the narrative without considering post-pandemic normalization.
Lesson: Temporary tailwinds don't justify permanent valuations. Always consider what happens when the catalyst reverses. Set clear exit strategies based on fundamentals, not hope.
ARKK ETF
Feb 2021 - Nov 2022
-58%
Bought into the "disruptive innovation" story at the top. Believed that high-growth tech would continue regardless of interest rates. Ignored the duration risk in high-multiple growth stocks when rates started rising.
Lesson: Understand macro conditions and their impact on different asset classes. Growth stocks are particularly sensitive to interest rate changes. Don't ignore the Fed when making investment decisions.
Various SPACs
2020 - 2021
-70%
Got swept up in the SPAC mania, buying multiple pre-revenue companies with grand promises. Ignored the misaligned incentives where sponsors get paid regardless of performance. Fell for marketing over substance.
Lesson: Understand the incentive structure of any investment vehicle. If the promoters get rich while investors lose money, avoid it. Stick to businesses with proven revenue and clear paths to profitability.
Options Trading
Various periods
-40%
Tried to time earnings and market moves with options. Made some big wins early which gave false confidence. Time decay and volatility crush taught me expensive lessons about the complexities of options pricing.
Lesson: Options are primarily insurance instruments, not speculation tools. The house edge favors sellers over buyers. Focus on building wealth through ownership of productive assets, not derivatives.
